Judul 1

The pineapple is indigenous to South America, where it has been cultivated for many centuries. The introduction of the pineapple plant to Europe in the 17th century made it a significant cultural icon of luxury. Pineapples grow as a small shrub; the individual flowers of the unpollinated plant fuse to form a multiple fruit.

Judul 2

The pineapple is a herbaceous perennial, which grows to 1.0 to 1.5 m tall on average. The plant has a short, stocky stem with tough, waxy leaves. When creating its fruit, it usually produces up to 200 flowers.
